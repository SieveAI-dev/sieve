Bech32 vs Bech32m: what changed in Bitcoin address encoding

Bech32 (BIP173):
- Used for P2WPKH (SegWit v0, native) and P2WSH
- All lowercase: bc1q...
- Human-readable part (HRP): "bc" for mainnet, "tb" for testnet
- Witness version prefix: q = version 0

Bech32m (BIP350):
- Used for P2TR (Taproot, SegWit v1+)
- All lowercase: bc1p...
- Same HRP structure but different checksum constant
- Prevents accidental cross-spending between v0 and v1+ scripts

Example Taproot address: bc1p5d7rjq7g6rdk2yhzks9smlaqtedr4dekq08ge8ztwac72sfr9rusxg3297
Note the "p" after "bc1" — distinguishes from P2WPKH "q".
