Address poisoning / phishing address attacks:

Attack pattern: attacker creates an address that looks similar to a frequently
used address in the victim's transaction history. They send dust to the victim's
address from the lookalike. The victim copies the wrong address from their history.

Example:
  Real:   0xd8dA6BF26964aF9D7eEd9e03E53415D37aA96045
  Fake:   0xd8dA000000000000000000000E53415D37aA96045 (first 6 / last 6 chars match)

Defense:
1. Always verify full address, not just prefix/suffix
2. Use address book / ENS rather than copying from tx history
3. Some wallets highlight character differences in red
4. Metamask address book with verified entries prevents this
