Kubernetes TLS secrets with PEM certificates (kubectl commands):

Create TLS secret from cert files:
  kubectl create secret tls my-tls-secret \
    --cert=path/to/tls.crt \
    --key=path/to/tls.key \
    -n my-namespace

The Secret stores:
  data.tls.crt: base64-encoded PEM certificate (BEGIN CERTIFICATE — public)
  data.tls.key: base64-encoded PEM private key (chmod 600 equivalent enforced by k8s RBAC)

View certificate (decode base64 to inspect):
  kubectl get secret my-tls-secret -o jsonpath='{.data.tls\.crt}' | base64 -d | openssl x509 -text -noout

Use in Ingress:
  spec:
    tls:
    - hosts: [example.com]
      secretName: my-tls-secret

cert-manager automates certificate rotation and creates/updates these secrets automatically.
