PEM format history: Originally defined in RFC 1421 (1993) for "Privacy Enhanced Mail," PEM was intended to secure email. The mail encryption use case largely failed, but the container format survived and became the de facto standard for TLS certificates and SSH keys. RFC 7468 (2015) formalized the "Textual Encodings of PKIX, PKCS, and CMS Structures" — i.e., the -----BEGIN/END----- framing we use today. RFC 7468 specifies strict base64 (no spaces in body, 64 chars per line, no extra headers), while older implementations sometimes added "Proc-Type" and "DEK-Info" headers for encrypted content.
