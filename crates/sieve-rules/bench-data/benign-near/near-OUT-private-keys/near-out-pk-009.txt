When a private key is passphrase-protected, its PEM label changes to "BEGIN ENCRYPTED PRIVATE KEY" (PKCS#8 encrypted form). The encryption algorithm and salt are encoded in the ASN.1 structure before the key material. To remove passphrase protection: openssl pkey -in encrypted.pem -out plain.pem (prompts for password). To add passphrase: openssl pkey -in plain.pem -out encrypted.pem -aes256. For PKCS#1 RSA format the header shows "Proc-Type: 4,ENCRYPTED" and "DEK-Info: AES-256-CBC,<IV>" as PEM headers before the base64 body.
