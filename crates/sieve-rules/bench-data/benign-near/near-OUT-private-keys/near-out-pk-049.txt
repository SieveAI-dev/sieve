TLS certificate chain structure — all "BEGIN CERTIFICATE" blocks (public information):

A full chain contains three certificates in order:
1. Leaf/server certificate — identifies your specific domain
2. Intermediate CA certificate — signed by root, signs leaf
3. Root CA certificate — trusted by browsers/OS (usually omitted from chain file)

Verify a chain with openssl:
  openssl verify -CAfile /etc/ssl/certs/ca-certificates.crt \
    -untrusted intermediate.pem leaf.pem

Check which CA signed a certificate:
  openssl x509 -in cert.pem -noout -issuer -subject

Download server certificate chain:
  openssl s_client -connect example.com:443 -showcerts < /dev/null 2>/dev/null \
    | awk '/BEGIN/,/END/{ print }' > chain.pem
