DSA (Digital Signature Algorithm) keys use the PEM label "BEGIN DSA PRIVATE KEY" in PKCS#1-equivalent format, or "BEGIN PRIVATE KEY" in PKCS#8 wrapping. DSA keys are rarely used today; ECDSA or EdDSA are preferred. To generate a DSA key (not recommended for new systems): openssl dsaparam -genkey 2048 | openssl dsa -out dsa_key.pem. Note that DSA is limited to 1024-bit per FIPS 186-2 for legacy systems, or 2048/3072 in FIPS 186-4. Most modern TLS stacks have dropped DSA support entirely.
