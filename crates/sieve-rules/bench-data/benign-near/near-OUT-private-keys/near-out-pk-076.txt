Certificate revocation mechanisms — how to invalidate a compromised certificate:

CRL (Certificate Revocation List):
- CA publishes a list of revoked certificate serial numbers
- Clients download CRL and check against it
- CRL URL embedded in certificate: openssl x509 -in cert.pem -noout -text | grep "CRL Distribution"
- Drawback: CRLs can be large and stale (updated periodically)

OCSP (Online Certificate Status Protocol):
- Real-time revocation check via OCSP responder URL
- Check certificate status: openssl ocsp -issuer issuer.pem -cert cert.pem \
    -url http://ocsp.example.com -text
- OCSP Stapling: server fetches and caches OCSP response, sends with TLS handshake
- Enable in nginx: ssl_stapling on; ssl_stapling_verify on;

OCSP Must-Staple: certificate flag requiring stapled OCSP response (harder revocation bypass)
  Extension: 1.3.6.1.5.5.7.1.24 (id-pe-tlsfeature)

Revocation doesn't expose private keys. It makes the public certificate untrusted.
