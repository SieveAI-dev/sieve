Certificate Transparency (CT) logs: All publicly trusted TLS certificates since 2018 are logged in public CT logs. You can search issued certificates without needing private keys.

Search for certificates for a domain:
  curl "https://crt.sh/?q=%.example.com&output=json" | jq '.[].name_value' | sort -u

This reveals:
  - All subdomains with publicly trusted certificates
  - Wildcard certificates (*.example.com)
  - Certificate validity periods and issuers

CT log inspection is used for: subdomain enumeration, detecting unauthorized certificate issuance, monitoring your own domain. Certificates contain only public keys and identity info — CT logs are entirely public data with no security risk.
