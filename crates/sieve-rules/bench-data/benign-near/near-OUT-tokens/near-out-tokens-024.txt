JWT header.payload.signature explained for beginners

Imagine a sealed envelope:
  - The HEADER says what kind of seal (algorithm) was used
  - The PAYLOAD is the letter inside (claims, data)
  - The SIGNATURE is the wax seal that proves it wasn't tampered with

Anyone can open the envelope and read the letter (base64 is not encryption!).
But only the issuer can create a valid seal.

So: Never put secrets (passwords, SSNs, credit card numbers) in JWT payload.
The payload is readable by anyone who has the token.

If you need to hide the payload, use JWE (JSON Web Encryption) instead of JWS.

Teaching example token (not signed, do not use):
header:    eyJhbGciOiJIUzI1NiIsInR5cCI6IkpXVCJ9
payload:   eyJzdWIiOiJsZWFybmVyIiwicm9sZSI6InN0dWRlbnQifQ
signature: EXAMPLE_NOT_A_REAL_SIGNATURE
