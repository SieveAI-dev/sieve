# Stripe Security: PCI Compliance

Using Stripe.js and the hosted fields keeps your servers out of PCI scope.
Never collect raw card numbers on your servers.

What Stripe.js does:
- Card numbers go directly to Stripe's servers, not yours
- Returns a token (tok_...) or PaymentMethod ID (pm_...) to your server
- Your server uses the token/PM ID to create charges—no card data

Your sk_test_* or sk_live_* key stays server-side.
Your pk_test_* or pk_live_* key is safe to include in JavaScript.

PCI SAQ A compliance achieved when:
  1. Using Stripe hosted fields or Stripe Checkout
  2. HTTPS on all pages
  3. No card data on your servers

For highest assurance: use Stripe Checkout (redirected to stripe.com).
