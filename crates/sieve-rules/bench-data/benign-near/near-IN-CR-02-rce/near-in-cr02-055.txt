# nc as a simple chat tool between two terminals

Terminal 1 (server):
    nc -l -p 4444

Terminal 2 (client):
    nc localhost 4444

Both terminals are now connected. Whatever you type in either terminal appears in the other. Press Ctrl+C or Ctrl+D to close the connection.

This is a classic sysadmin trick for testing TCP connectivity between processes on the same machine or across a local network. No shell is spawned, no commands are executed remotely—it's just a bidirectional byte stream.
