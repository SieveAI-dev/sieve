# dd: understanding block size (bs) performance impact

Block size significantly affects dd throughput:

Small bs (slow):
    dd bs=512 count=2097152 if=/dev/zero of=test.bin
    # 2M ops of 512 bytes = 1 GB total; many syscalls, slow

Optimal bs (fast):
    dd bs=4M count=256 if=/dev/zero of=test.bin
    # 256 ops of 4 MB = 1 GB total; fewer syscalls, fast

Rule of thumb: bs=1M to bs=4M gives good throughput for most storage types. Larger bs doesn't help much and wastes memory. SSDs may benefit from bs=64M for sequential writes.

The output file here is test.bin in the current directory. All writes go to a regular file, not a device.
