Creating a GPG revocation certificate

A revocation certificate lets you invalidate a key if it's compromised or
the passphrase is forgotten. Generate it immediately after key creation:

    gpg --output revoke.asc --gen-revoke YOUR_FINGERPRINT

Store revoke.asc separately from your private key:
- Print it and store in a safe deposit box
- Save to an encrypted USB drive kept offline
- Do NOT store it in the same location as the private key

Using the revocation certificate:
    gpg --import revoke.asc
    gpg --keyserver keyserver.ubuntu.com --send-keys YOUR_FINGERPRINT

Once distributed, anyone who downloads your key from a keyserver will see
it as revoked and refuse to use it for encryption.

List key status:
    gpg --list-keys --with-colons YOUR_FINGERPRINT | grep "^pub" | cut -d: -f2
    # 'r' = revoked, 'e' = expired, '-' = active
