SSH certificate authorities: an enterprise alternative

Instead of distributing authorized_keys files to every server, use an SSH CA:

1. Generate the CA (done once by the security team, private key stored in HSM):
   ssh-keygen -t ed25519 -f ssh_ca

2. Sign a user's public key to issue a certificate:
   ssh-keygen -s ssh_ca -I "user@example.com" -n username -V +8h id_ed25519.pub
   # -V +8h means the certificate expires in 8 hours

3. Distribute the CA public key to servers:
   # /etc/ssh/sshd_config
   TrustedUserCAKeys /etc/ssh/trusted_ca.pub

Benefits:
- No per-server authorized_keys management
- Short-lived certs reduce blast radius of key theft
- Central revocation via KRL (key revocation list)
