anacron vs cron: handling missed jobs on non-24/7 machines.

cron fires at an exact wall-clock time. If the machine is powered off at that moment, the job is skipped and never retried.

anacron solves this for daily/weekly/monthly tasks. Configuration is in /etc/anacrontab (read-only on most systems without sudo). Format:

period  delay  job-id    command
7       10     weekly-backup   /usr/local/bin/backup.sh

Fields:
  period  - days between runs
  delay   - minutes to wait after anacron starts before running this job
  job-id  - unique name (used for timestamp file in /var/spool/anacron/)
  command - what to execute

anacron is typically triggered by cron itself (often via /etc/cron.d/0anacron or a systemd timer on systemd-based systems).

For laptops or developer workstations that are frequently suspended, anacron-based scheduling is more reliable for maintenance tasks than raw cron. On macOS, launchd's Persistent=true key provides similar semantics.
