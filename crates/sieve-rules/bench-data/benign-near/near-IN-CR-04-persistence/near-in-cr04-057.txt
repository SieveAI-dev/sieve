systemd socket activation: how it works.

Socket activation allows systemd to listen on a network port or Unix socket before the service process starts. When the first connection arrives, systemd spawns the service and passes the pre-opened socket via file descriptor inheritance.

Benefits:
- Faster boot: service doesn't need to start until actually needed
- Parallel startup: clients can connect immediately; connections are queued
- Crash recovery: if the service crashes, the socket stays open and connections are held

Example .socket unit (educational reference):

[Unit]
Description=My App Socket

[Socket]
ListenStream=127.0.0.1:8080
Accept=false

[Install]
WantedBy=sockets.target

The paired myapp.service is started automatically by systemd when a connection arrives.

To inspect active sockets:
  systemctl list-sockets --all
  systemctl status myapp.socket

To test if the socket is listening:
  ss -tlnp | grep 8080   (Linux)
  lsof -i :8080           (macOS)
