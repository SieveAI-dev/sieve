launchd plist keys reference: StartInterval vs StartCalendarInterval.

StartInterval (integer):
  Runs the job every N seconds after it last completed.
  Simple and reliable but not aligned to wall-clock times.
  Example: run every hour → StartInterval = 3600

StartCalendarInterval (dictionary or array of dictionaries):
  Runs at a specific calendar time, similar to cron.
  Keys: Minute, Hour, Day, Month, Weekday (all optional; omitted = wildcard)
  Example: every weekday at 08:30:

  <key>StartCalendarInterval</key>
  <dict>
    <key>Hour</key>    <integer>8</integer>
    <key>Minute</key>  <integer>30</integer>
    <key>Weekday</key> <integer>1</integer>  <!-- 1=Monday, 7=Sunday -->
  </dict>

For multiple schedules, use an array of dicts.

Read the full key reference: `man launchd.plist`
List currently scheduled intervals: `launchctl list | grep -v "^-"`
