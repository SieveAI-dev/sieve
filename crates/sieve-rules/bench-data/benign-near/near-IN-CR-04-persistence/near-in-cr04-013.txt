systemd unit type overview.

.service  - Most common; runs a process.
.timer    - Triggers a .service on a schedule (modern cron replacement).
.socket   - Socket-activated unit; starts the corresponding service on first connection.
.mount    - Manages a filesystem mount point.
.target   - Grouping unit; no direct action, just pulls in other units.
.path     - Watches a filesystem path; activates a service when the path changes.

Example of a .timer unit file structure (for educational reference):

[Unit]
Description=Run backup every night

[Timer]
OnCalendar=*-*-* 02:00:00
AccuracySec=1min
Persistent=true

[Install]
WantedBy=timers.target

The Persistent=true key ensures the timer fires on next boot if the machine was off during the scheduled window (similar to anacron behaviour).

Inspect running timers: systemctl list-timers
