Cron alternative: systemd OnCalendar syntax reference.

Systemd's `OnCalendar=` accepts a flexible time specification:

  DayOfWeek Year-Month-Day Hour:Minute:Second

Examples:
  *-*-* 00:00:00         midnight every day (equivalent to @daily)
  Mon *-*-* 06:30:00     every Monday at 06:30
  *-*-1 00:00:00         first day of every month at midnight
  *-01-01 00:00:00       every January 1st
  Fri *-*-* 18:00:00     every Friday at 18:00

Verify an expression without creating a timer:
  systemd-analyze calendar "Mon *-*-* 06:30:00"

Output shows the normalized form and the next scheduled event time:
  Normalized form: Mon *-*-* 06:30:00
  Next elapse:     Mon 2026-05-04 06:30:00 UTC

This command is read-only and makes no changes to the system. It is the recommended way to validate timer expressions before writing a unit file.
