macOS System Settings: managing Login Items through the GUI.

macOS Ventura (13) and later moved Login Items management to:
  System Settings > General > Login Items & Extensions

The panel shows two sections:
1. "Open at Login" — apps that launch in the foreground when you log in
2. "Allow in the Background" — background items registered via SMAppService

To add a login item through the GUI:
1. Open System Settings
2. Navigate to General > Login Items & Extensions
3. Click the "+" button under "Open at Login"
4. Navigate to /Applications and select the desired app
5. Click Open

To remove a login item: select it in the list and click the "−" button.

Note: this GUI approach is fully user-controlled and transparent. The system records the item in a system database managed by launchd's bootstrap server, not in a plist file that you can accidentally misconfigure.

Apple documentation: "Customizing startup items" — support.apple.com
