Reviewing an existing LaunchAgent plist for correctness.

Before accepting a LaunchAgent from a third-party installer, it is good practice to read and understand the plist. To read a plist in human-readable XML format:

  cat ~/Library/LaunchAgents/com.example.myapp.plist

Or for binary plists:
  plutil -p ~/Library/LaunchAgents/com.example.myapp.plist

Key fields to check when reviewing:
1. Label: should match the filename (minus .plist extension)
2. ProgramArguments: are the paths absolute? Does the binary exist?
3. RunAtLoad: will this run immediately on load? (check if that's expected)
4. KeepAlive: will this restart continuously? (appropriate for daemons, not one-shots)
5. EnvironmentVariables: are there any unexpected variables being set?
6. StartCalendarInterval: does the schedule make sense for what the app claims to do?

Red flags in a third-party plist:
- ProgramArguments pointing to a script in /tmp or a user-writable path
- EnvironmentVariables setting DYLD_INSERT_LIBRARIES
- KeepAlive with no ThrottleInterval (can spin CPU)

Inspect only; do not modify plist files directly unless you are the author.
