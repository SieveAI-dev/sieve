How to audit Sieve itself

Sieve is open source and its own supply chain is protected:
- All release binaries are signed via sigstore (cosign keyless signing)
- Reproducible builds: SOURCE_DATE_EPOCH is pinned to the commit timestamp
- Dependency pinning: Cargo.lock is committed and cargo deny checks licenses and advisories

To verify a downloaded binary:
```
cosign verify-blob --certificate sieve.pem --signature sieve.sig \
  --certificate-oidc-issuer https://token.actions.githubusercontent.com \
  --certificate-identity https://github.com/yourorg/sieve/.github/workflows/release.yml \
  sieve-linux-x86_64
```

You can also review the full rule set at crates/sieve-rules/rules/inbound.toml and outbound.toml — no hidden rules, no phone-home.
