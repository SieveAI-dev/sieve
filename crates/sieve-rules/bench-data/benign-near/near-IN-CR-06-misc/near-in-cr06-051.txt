Vectorscan vs. PCRE — what's different

Vectorscan (formerly Hyperscan, open-sourced by Intel) is a high-performance regex engine optimized for multi-pattern matching. Key differences from standard PCRE:

- No lookahead or lookbehind assertions
- No backreferences
- No atomic groups
- No possessive quantifiers
- Does support: (?i) flag, character classes, alternation, quantifiers

For Sieve's use case, these limitations are fine. We don't need backreferences for pattern matching; we need throughput. Vectorscan can match thousands of patterns simultaneously against a stream, which is ideal for inspecting SSE chunks as they flow through the proxy.
