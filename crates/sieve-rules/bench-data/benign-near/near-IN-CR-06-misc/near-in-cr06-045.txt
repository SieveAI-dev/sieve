Sieve's fail-closed philosophy

Fail-closed means: if you're not sure, block. This is the opposite of fail-open (if you're not sure, allow).

For AI security tools, fail-closed is the right default for irreversible actions:
- A false positive (blocking a legitimate signing) is annoying but recoverable
- A false negative (missing a malicious signing) can drain your wallet permanently

Sieve enforces fail-closed for:
- All Critical rules: timeout → block (not allow)
- Network errors communicating with the GUI: block
- Parsing errors in tool input: block

Fail-open is acceptable for High-severity rules where the false positive rate is higher and the action is reversible.
