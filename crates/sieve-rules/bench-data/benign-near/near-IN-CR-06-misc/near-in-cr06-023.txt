Constitutional AI and its limits

Anthropic's Constitutional AI (CAI) trains models to follow a set of principles through self-critique and revision. The model learns to evaluate its own outputs against principles like "don't help with harm."

CAI is effective for many safety properties but has known limitations:
- It operates at training time, not inference time — a model can still be manipulated through carefully crafted prompts
- It doesn't prevent misuse by the system integrator (an app can pass harmful context to the model)
- It doesn't address the "excessive agency" problem — a model can be "safe" in its responses but still execute harmful actions through tools

Sieve complements CAI by adding a runtime action filter that doesn't depend on the model's own judgment.
