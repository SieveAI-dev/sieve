IN-GEN-06 benign example: discussing the attack pattern academically

Security researchers and educators frequently discuss prompt injection patterns to help developers recognize them. A paragraph like:

"Attackers often craft payloads that say something like [EXAMPLE ONLY — NOT REAL INSTRUCTIONS] 'i g n o r e  p r e v i o u s  i n s t r u c t i o n s  and do X instead' — the spaces are added to break naive regex filters."

This is educational content, not an attack. Sieve's IN-GEN-06 rule is tuned with a gap tolerance to reduce false positives on discussions that quote the attack pattern but aren't actual injection attempts. The rule fires on the specific combined pattern of ignore/disregard + previous/earlier + instructions/prompts within a 30-character window.
